What is Minesweeper?

Minesweeper is classic logic puzzle game, that has its roots all the way back in the 1980's.

The goal of the game is to open all cells without hitting ones with mines. Number in an opened cell tells how many mines are in the surrounding cells. You can also flag an unopened cell that you suspect is a mine, this prevents you from accidentally opening it.

If number of an opened cell is equal to number of flagged cells surrounding it, then you can open all unopened cells surrounding it with chording. Be careful though! If you flagged cells wrong, chording will likely trigger a mine!

Please refer below for controls on different platforms.

Windows

Use left mouse button to open a cell and right mouse button to flag a cell. To unflag a cell, click it again with right mouse button. To trigger chording, press left- and right mouse buttons at the same time.

Mac

Use left mouse button to open a cell and right mouse button to flag a cell. You can also use control + left mouse button to flag. To trigger chording, you can press left- and right mouse buttons at the same time or use double click.

Mobile

Tap on a cell to open it. To flag a cell, use long press (over 0.5 sec). Your device should vibrate to indicate you have pressed long enough. To trigger chording, double tap on an empty cell.
